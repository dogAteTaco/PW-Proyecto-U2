The Long Dark Blue by Swain – Favorite Albums

Swain is a band I discovered through the magic of YouTube recommendations. The band went with the name This Routine is Hell for years, but decided to change their name along with their sound. TRIH was a hardcore punk-rock band but now they wanted to experiment with softer music and different styles. While I do like the TRIH albums I do admit they could get a little repetitive like most punk-rock bands so this experimenting was nice to see. So let’s talk about one of my favorite albums and the the band’s first album as Swain, The Long Dark Blue.

As a whole, the album is about someone going through tough times, without a clear mind of what they want to do and in a way being too stuck to do those anything to achieve their goals. Musically speaking the style is a mix of good old grunge with some Weezer vibes on top of the hardcore punk style from their previous work. While some of some songs on the album may be very different than others, the heavily distorted guitar, thick bass tone and harsh vocals on the calmer songs make it blend pretty well with the harder songs, and those mellow slow songs prevent it from getting too stale.

The album starts with “Hold My Head” and it doesn’t hold back anything. It has catchy vocal melodies, trashy guitars, simple but groovy drums, intense screaming with a nice background harmony; elements that will come back throughout the album. Like most of the other tracks, these lyrics are almost straight with their meaning; this one in particular is about having a shitty day and looking for some magic trick to fix this feeling. While short I think is my favorite track on the record just because it does everything that is great in this album perfectly.

Next is “Half Asleep/Half Awake” which is clearly about living a half-life, lost and not sure what to do with your life. While fun and catchy is one of my least favorite songs in the record.

After that is “Punk Rock Messed You Up”, this being the first song I’ve heard from them. The title says a lot about the lyrics, it works as a conversation; I see the verses as society telling a kid about what “messed him up”’; things like parents, school but most of all that satanic punk-rock music. On the other side of the conversation are the verses, being a sarcastic or unaware response saying “I’m totally ok” (and I say sarcastic because the whole album is about being a bad place, not doing the things one wants, which is not exactly being ok). Aside from the very well handled lyrics as two sides of a conversation, the song also mixed that punk-rock screamed verses with nice clear vocals on the chorus giving some contrast on the song and leaving a clear separation between the two sides.

So, like I said in the beginning, there’s Weezer vibes throughout the album, and “Never Clear My Room” is the clearest example. A very laid back rock song with only clean vocals with themes of someone knowing they are in a bad place (“filled under lost“) and telling everyone you might as well give up on them and “save yourself the trouble” because they’re hopeless. While the guitar may sound a little repetitive and monotone on the verses I think it does an excellent job on trapping you in the atmosphere. Also I love the chorus, I love songs that sound slow and fast at the same time.

The next song is “It’s Hard to Make Friends”, while I liked it, is nothing too memorable but has a place in the album, being a nice bridge to “Kiss Me Hard”, a fun sounding song with a catchy chorus.

Then comes “Faze Me”, the first song that sound like a left out of TRIH; and I don’t say it in a bad way since is a nice addition that prevents the album from sounding too poppy. It starts with that guitar feedback that was used, sometimes too much, in TRIH songs and has no clear vocals apart from that chorus on the end of the song. Lyrically is my favorite song in the album, it talks about half-assing everything and not taking risks. The chorus “If it doesn’t scare you you’re not doing it right, if it doesn’t faze you you’re doing nothing at all” summarizes the message of the song and the whole album perfect: take risks and leave your comfort zone, it may be scary but is always worth it.

Again with the Weezer vibes we have two slow alternative songs: “Secrets Inside” and “Seen a Good Man (In a bad mood)”; the later having a dose of Nirvana in it, especially in the verses. While some may say the next track (“You’re Not Special”) is a filler song, I would strongly disagree; while it may not be a worthy song to listen to separate from the album, it works within it as a great transition and creates contrast to the next song, “Hoping for it”. Another THID sounding song with straight to the point, no bullshit lyrics; it talks about wanting to be something and doing nothing to get there but at the same time feeling frustration for not being able to just do it. This is, next to “Faze Me”, the heaviest the album gets.

The next song is “Strange Way Down”. There’s no other song that sounds more “being done with life” than this; a slow bored sickening riff with a repetitive bass line which are complemented with a lazy singing voice. The only complain I have with this song is that I would have preferred if the dreamy slow sound would have continued through the whole song instead of having that heavy verse in the later part of the song, but still I love it and the atmosphere it creates, and when it comes back to that slow rhythm the lyrics complement the repetitive “boring” music perfectly (I say boring because it sound like they would rather be playing something else but is not boring, is just the feeling the song goes for).

Last song in the album is “Rid Myself of You”, which continues with the mix of dreamy slow rock of the previous track. In my opinion, a perfect closing track for this album after switching between heavy and soft music. Apart from the music being a good closure, the lyrics talk about getting rid of “the long dark blue”; that meaning being sick and getting rid of the depression that the person behind all these songs feels.

While this is not a happy album by any means I like the message of it quite a lot; try new things and don’t kick yourself in the teeth. It may not be the most radio friendly but I think there’s a lot of fun (ignoring the lyrics) and catchy songs. In my opinion there’s not a single track that feels out of place or overstays its welcome; all great quick songs that have a clear message and for what the band was trying to achieve I think is an easy 10/10 and easily one of my most played albums.

If you liked this album, the band has stated on their FB page they were working on a new album so you have that to look forward to. Also check their bandcamp page and buy their album if you have the chance.

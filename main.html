Making a Custom Kindle Dictionary

Making a Custom Kindle Dictionary isn’t very hard to do but while researching how to and doing a custom one I found a lot of small problems and incomplete or outdated info, so here is how I did it and the problems I’ve found while doing a Tolkien/English Dictionary.

In this blog, I’m gonna explain to you how to construct the dictionary vocabulary, some problems I came across, how to do some formatting if you wanna be fancy about it and how to add and use the dictionary in your Kindle. We are going to make use of the Command Prompt, download a GitHub and run a Python script, if you aren’t familiar with them don’t worry since you only need to install some stuff and write very basic commands and I’ll explain them on the go.

This was made with the Kindle Firmware 5.14.3.0.1, Kindle Previewer 3.66.0, Tab2Opf Feb 3 2015, and Python 3.9.1, so if you find any differences comment and I’ll try to add them to this guide but since most of the info I’ve found online is pretty similar you shouldn’t have any problems

The Long Dark Blue by Swain – Favorite Albums

Swain is a band I discovered through the magic of YouTube recommendations. The band went with the name This Routine is Hell for years, but decided to change their name along with their sound. TRIH was a hardcore punk-rock band but now they wanted to experiment with softer music and different styles. While I do like the TRIH albums I do admit they could get a little repetitive like most punk-rock bands so this experimenting was nice to see. So let’s talk about one of my favorite albums and the the band’s first album as Swain, The Long Dark Blue.

As a whole, the album is about someone going through tough times, without a clear mind of what they want to do and in a way being too stuck to do those anything to achieve their goals. Musically speaking the style is a mix of good old grunge with some Weezer vibes on top of the hardcore punk style from their previous work. While some of some songs on the album may be very different than others, the heavily distorted guitar, thick bass tone and harsh vocals on the calmer songs make it blend pretty well with the harder songs, and those mellow slow songs prevent it from getting too stale.

The album starts with “Hold My Head” and it doesn’t hold back anything. It has catchy vocal melodies, trashy guitars, simple but groovy drums, intense screaming with a nice background harmony; elements that will come back throughout the album. Like most of the other tracks, these lyrics are almost straight with their meaning; this one in particular is about having a shitty day and looking for some magic trick to fix this feeling. While short I think is my favorite track on the record just because it does everything that is great in this album perfectly.
